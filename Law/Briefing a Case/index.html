Learn Notes

Lucie vs Zehmer is a good beginner case

Parties

  • Platintiff : Lucy
  • Defendant: Zehmer

Issue

Does Zehmer have to hand over the farm if he thought the deal was a joke but acted as if it wasn’t?

Procedural Posture

Appealing a decree by a lower court order that zehmer doesn’t have to turn the farm over to lucy. Lucy is seeking “specific performance” (wants the farm).

Facts for Zehmer

  • Lucy brought whiskey with him; Zehmer and Lucy were drinking. A waitress testified that the men were “drinking right much”
  • Zehmer says he thought this was a joke; whispered to his wife that it was a joke
  • The conversation sounded as if it was in jest
  • Zehmer write out the contract on the back of a restaurant check - very informal
  • Zehmer refused to take Lucy’s $5 to bind the bargain, saying “this is liquor talking, I don’t want ot sell the farm”
  • Zehmer sent Lucy a letter saying he’d never agreed to sell

Facts for Lucy

  • Lucy had tried to buy the place before
  • Zehmer actually put the contract in writing himself
  • The contract states in clear terms the sale
  • Zehmer re-wrote the contract, changing it from I to We When Lucy pointed out that Mrz Zehmer would need to sign it too
  • Both Zehmers signed the contract
  • The discussion about the contract was lengthy
  • Lucy didn’t feel drunk and said Zehmer didn’t seem drunk
  • Lucy immediately arranged financing and checked title

Who Wins

Lucy

Holding

Zehmer acted like he wanted to sell, so it doesn’t matter what he was really thinking. Zehmer has to convey the farm to Lucy.

Reasoning

There was an offer, acceptance, execution and delivery of the written contract. The law doesn’t require the mental assent of the parties - In other words, Zehmers thoughts are irrelevant if Lucy didn’t know what he was thinking. If you could get out of a contract by saying that you didn’t really mean it, then all contracts would be unenforceable.
