Shellshock

Blackwood

Taking the chance, I roll out from under the creature, ignoring the pulsing ache that covers my ribs. Tufts of fur and dirt cling to the edge of my dagger as I sheathe it, looking back to see the bear following behind me. My throat constricts, and I can feel my stomach clench as I run, sprinting fast enough that the bitter chill starts to stiffen my hair. Two more arrows thud into the bear, and it turns, echoing out a furious growl.
